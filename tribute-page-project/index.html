George Roger Waters

The man who built The Wall

  • 1943: Waters is born on Septermber 6th to Mary Whyte and Eric Fletcher Waters.


  • 1944: on February 18th his father is killed at Aprilia, during the battle of Anzio; this event will mark Roger's imagination and creativity for the rest of his life.


  • 1965: he cofounds the English progressive Rock band Pink Floyd, together with Roger Keith Barrett, Nick Mason and Richard Wright.


  • 1973-1979: Pink Floyd achieve international success with concept albums The Dark side of the Moon, Wish You Were Here, Animals and The Wall.


  • 1983: The Final Cut, an album almost totally engineered by waters, sees the light. It is the last of Pink Floyd's albums as a quartet. Shortly after, Waters leaves the band to start a solo career.


  • 1990: stages one of the greatest rock concerts in history, The Wall- Live in Berlin
